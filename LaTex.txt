\begin{abstract}
The Hubble tension, representing an $\sim 8.4\%$ discrepancy between early-universe and late-universe measurements of the Hubble constant $H_0$, stands as one of the most significant challenges in modern cosmology. We present the **Unified Applicable Time (UAT)** framework, which resolves this tension through quantum gravitational effects derived from Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG). The UAT framework introduces a fundamental parameter $k_{\text{early}}$ that modifies the early universe expansion history, reducing the sound horizon $r_d$ by approximately \RdReduction{} while maintaining the locally measured $H_0$. Comprehensive Bayesian MCMC analysis on combined CMB, BAO, and SNe Ia data yields **Decisive Evidence** for UAT over $\Lambda$CDM ($\ln B_{01} = \BayesFactor$) and an excellent overall fit ($\chi^2 = \ChiSqFinal$, corresponding to a $\Delta\chi^2 = \DeltaChiSqFinal$ improvement over the optimal $\Lambda$CDM model). This work provides a physically motivated and statistically robust solution to the $H_0$ tension.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
The Hubble constant ($H_0$) discrepancy, reaching beyond $5\sigma$, requires new physics beyond the standard $\Lambda$CDM model \cite{Planck_2018, SH0ES}. The core issue is the fundamental incompatibility between the predicted sound horizon ($r_d$) at last scattering and the locally measured expansion rate.

We propose the **Unified Applicable Time (UAT)** framework \cite{UAT_Original}, which is rooted in Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG) \cite{LQG} principles. The UAT mechanism, termed the **Torsion Ring Hypothesis**, introduces a small but crucial modification to the early universe expansion rate, quantified by the parameter $k_{\text{early}}$.

% ADDITION: Differentiation from Alternative Solutions
\subsection{Differentiation from Alternative Solutions}

The UAT framework distinguishes itself from popular early-time solutions, such as Early Dark Energy (EDE) models, in two key aspects. First, UAT is derived from a **first-principles quantum gravity hypothesis (LQG)**, providing a microphysical origin for the modification, rather than relying on a phenomenological scalar field. Second, unlike EDE models, UAT's modification parameter, $k_{\text{early}}$, **multiplies the energy density components** of radiation and matter (Eq. \ref{eq:uat_phenomenological}), directly altering the geometry's response to energy, which naturally leads to the reduced sound horizon $r_d$ required for the tension resolution.

\section{Theoretical Foundation and Methodology}

\subsection{Microphysical Foundation and Torsion Rings}
The UAT framework's core premise is that the universe's expansion must be corrected by quantum geometric effects relevant at the Planck scale. This correction emerges from unifying the cosmological scale factor, relativistic terms (Schwarzschild radius), and Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG) effects, particularly the **Torsion Ring Hypothesis**.

In the UAT model, **Torsion Rings** are discrete geometric excitations of spacetime, analogous to the flux tubes in LQG, which introduce a non-zero **torsion** field in the gravitational connection. During the ultra-high-density regime of the early universe ($z \gg 300$), these torsion effects become dominant. Their collective action manifests as an **effective energy density correction** that counteracts the standard expansion rate. This mechanism yields the complex foundational equation:

% EQUATION 1 (FIXED FOR TWO-COLUMN OVERFLOW using split)
\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
t_{\text{UAT}} = & t_{\text{event}} \times \frac{1}{a(t)} \times \frac{1}{\max\left(\sqrt{1-\frac{2GM(t)}{c^2 r}}, \frac{l_{\text{Planck}}}{r}\right)^2} \\
& \times \frac{1}{1 + \gamma\frac{l_{\text{Planck}}^2}{4\pi r_s^2} + \frac{d_L}{c}}
\end{split}
\label{eq:uat_foundational}
\end{equation}
The parameter $k_{\text{early}}$ is the phenomenological representation of the full quantum-torsional effect.

\subsection{Phenomenological Implementation ($k_{\text{early}}$)}
For computational tractability, the net effect of the Torsion Ring mechanism is parameterized by $k_{\text{early}}$ in the Friedmann equation:

% EQUATION 2 (FIXED FOR TWO-COLUMN OVERFLOW using split)
\begin{equation}
\begin{split}
E_{\text{UAT}}(z,k_{\text{early}})^2 = & k_{\text{early}} \cdot \Omega_{r,0}(1+z)^4 \\
& + k_{\text{early}} \cdot \Omega_{m,0}(1+z)^3 + \Omega_{\Lambda,0}
\end{split}
\label{eq:uat_phenomenological}
\end{equation}
This specific implementation ensures that the model preserves the late-time physics of $\Lambda$CDM ($\Omega_{\Lambda,0}$ is unaffected) while addressing the early-time physics governing $r_d$. The constrained result $k_{\text{early}} = \KEarlyOptimoMCMC < 1$ directly confirms the Torsion Ring mechanism acts to **reduce the effective expansion rate**.

\subsection{Sound Horizon Modification}
The core resolution mechanism is the reduction of the sound horizon ($r_d$) due to the modified expansion rate $H_{\text{UAT}}(z)$:

\begin{equation}
r^{\text{UAT}}_d = \int_{z_d}^{\infty} \frac{c}{H_{\text{UAT}}(z) \cdot a(z)} dz
\label{eq:uat_sound_horizon}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Statistical Analysis}
Two complementary analyses were performed using the source code `UAT\_realistic\_analysis\_14\_10\_25.py`:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item **Dynamic $\chi^2$ Analysis:** Minimizing $\chi^2$ against BAO data (BOSS DR12 \cite{BAO_BOSS_DR12} and eBOSS DR16 \cite{BAO_eBOSS_DR16}) while fixing $H_0 = 73.00$ km/s/Mpc and varying $k_{\text{early}}$.
    \item **Bayesian MCMC Analysis:** A full parameter estimation combining CMB (Planck), BAO, and SNe Ia (Pantheon+) data to derive the final constraints and Bayesian Evidence \cite{MCMC_UAT}.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Physical Implications}
% ---

\subsection{The Torsion Ring Hypothesis}
The constrained value $k_{\text{early}} = \KEarlyOptimoMCMC < 1$ is the central finding. This parameter directly implements the **Torsion Ring Hypothesis**. In physical terms:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item **Geometric Origin:** Torsion is a geometric property of spacetime related to spin or rotation. The "rings" refer to the discrete, quantized structures of spacetime (the "loops" of LQG) that, when excited, introduce a torsion field.
    \item **Decelerating Effect:** This torsion field effectively acts as a negative contribution to the total energy density $\rho$ in the Friedmann equation during the early universe. The effective energy density becomes $\rho_{\text{eff}} = \rho_{\text{std}} - \rho_{\text{torsion}}$.
    \item **Expansion Modification:** The overall result is a slower expansion rate ($H_{\text{UAT}} < H_{\Lambda\text{CDM}}$) prior to recombination, as visually confirmed by Figure \ref{fig:expansion_plot}. The parameter $k_{\text{early}} \approx 0.970$ means the energy density of radiation and matter was effectively reduced by $\KReduction$ due to the quantum gravitational friction of the Torsion Rings.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Resolution Mechanism}
The slower expansion rate directly reduces the time available for sound waves to propagate before recombination, leading to the predicted sound horizon $r_d = \RdUAT$ Mpc. This required $\RdReduction$ reduction in $r_d$ is the essential link that reconciles the high local $H_0$ with the observed angular scale of the CMB.

\section{Conclusion}
The **Unified Applicable Time (UAT)** framework, driven by the **Torsion Ring Hypothesis**, offers a physically motivated and highly successful resolution to the Hubble tension. The model achieves an excellent fit ($\chi^2 = \ChiSqFinal$) and provides overwhelming statistical evidence ($\ln B_{01} = \BayesFactor$) over the standard $\Lambda$CDM model.
